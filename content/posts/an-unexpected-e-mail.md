Today, while at the <a title="ATK/AT-SPI hackfest" href="https://live.gnome.org/Hackfests/ATK2011">ATK/AT-SPI hackfest</a>, I received a mail in my INBOX from a well known guy from the GNOME community stating just the following:
<blockquote><tt><span style="color: #000000">Don't know if you read Hackers news, but this one is pretty nice:</span></tt>
<tt><span style="color: #000000"><a href="http://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=2532790">http://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=2532790</a></span></tt>
<tt><span style="color: #000000">-- </span></tt>
<tt><span style="color: #000000">Regards</span></tt></blockquote>
<span style="color: #000000">So I followed the link and I found a nice thread titled <em>"Love in GIT"</em> where people were commenting about something which remind me of <a title="Love in Git, as some people calls it" href="http://git.gnome.org/browse/frogr/commit/?id=d20b4b7679a64349c09b611b6376f20bfa4fb1d2">one of the last commits I did</a> in <a title="Frogr" href="https://live.gnome.org/Frogr">frogr</a>, which became my favourite one so far in the project, by the way.</span>

<span style="color: #000000">But the big surprise for me came when I realized that the thread was actually about the commit I innocently made last week, when I realized that the so-much-important <a title="Frogr's THANKS file" href="http://git.gnome.org/browse/frogr/tree/THANKS">THANKS file</a> was outdated since almost one year ago, right when I got married, so I decided to update it. Then I understood that there was actually a good reason for the thread to remind me of that change... Yes, I know, sometimes I'm "a little bit too slow" :-)</span>

<span style="color: #000000">So I'd just like to publicly say thanks to the misterious-but-well-known-hacker that sent me the e-mail for making me draw a big smile on my face, and to the people commenting in that thread for the so nice (and funny... "make babies"??) messages posted in there.</span>

<span style="color: #000000">Thanks!</span>
