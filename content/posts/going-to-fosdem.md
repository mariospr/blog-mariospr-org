It's been two years since the <a href="/2015/01/08/frogr-0-11-released/">last time</a> I went to <a href="https://fosdem.org">FOSDEM</a>, but it seems that this year I'm going to be there again and, after having traveled to Brussels a few times already by plane and train, this year I'm going by car!: from home to the <a href="https://www.eurotunnel.com/build/">Euro tunnel</a> and then all the way up to Brussels. Let's see how it goes.

<a href="https://fosdem.org/2017/"><img class="aligncenter" src="https://fosdem.org/2017/support/promote/wide.png" alt="FOSDEM 2017" width="468" height="60" /></a>

As for the conference, I don't have any particular plan other than going to some keynotes and probably spending most of my time in the <a href="https://fosdem.org/2017/schedule/track/distributions/">Distributions</a> and the <a href="https://fosdem.org/2017/schedule/track/desktops/">Desktops</a> devrooms. Well, and of course <a href="https://wiki.gnome.org/Events/FOSDEM/2017">joining other GNOME people</a> at <a href="http://www.alabecasse.com/en/info.html">A La Bécasse</a>, on Saturday night.

As you might expect, I will have my <a href="https://endlessos.com/">Endless</a> laptop with me while in the conference, so feel free to come and say "hi" in case you're curious or want to talk about that if you see me around.

At the moment, I'm mainly focused on developing and improving our <a href="http://flatpak.org/">flatpak</a> story, how we deliver apps to our users via this wonderful piece of technology and how the overall user experience ends up being, so I'd be more than happy to chat/hack around this topic and/or about how we integrate <a href="http://flatpak.org/">flatpak</a> in <a href="https://endlessos.com/">EndlessOS</a>, the challenges we found, the solutions we implemented... and so forth.

That said, <a href="http://flatpak.org/">flatpak</a> is one of my many development hats in <a href="https://endlessos.com/">Endless</a>, so be sure I'm open to talk about many other things, including not work-related ones, of course.

Now, if you excuse me, I have a bag to prepare, an English car to "adapt" for the journey ahead and, more importantly, quite some hours to sleep. Tomorrow it will be a long day, but it will be worth it.

See you at <a href="https://fosdem.org">FOSDEM</a>!
