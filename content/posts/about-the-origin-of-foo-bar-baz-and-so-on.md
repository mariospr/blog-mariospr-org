I recently felt a strange curiosity about the actual origin of those typical words that are "foo", "bar" and "baz", commonly used in computer-related issues such as programming examples. I already knew that they were a wide used way of naming example functions, params... but I really wanted to deeply know about the origin of them, so I googled for a while and I finally reached <a target="_blank" title="Foobar wikipedia definition" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foobar">the wikipedia topic explaining this concept</a>.

Once there, it was a surprise for me knowing about some theories about this, as that one which speculates about foobar to be a phonological interpretation of the first letters of the <a title="Runic alphabet" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Runic_alphabet">Runic alphabet</a>. However, the fact which was more surprisingly for me was the existence of an actual <a title="R.F.C. (Request For Comments)" target="_blank" href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Request_for_Comments">RFC</a> talking about this issue: <a title="RFC 3092: Etymology of " target="_blank" href="http://www.faqs.org/rfcs/rfc3092.html">RFC 3092</a>.

I must confess I wasn't able to stand without taking a brief look into it and I think it's really funny to read some of the explanations around the foo term, and that's why I wrote this post: for all the people who, like me, don't know the meaning of these strange words yet and want to know.
