As my mate <a title="API's blog" href="https://blogs.igalia.com/apinheiro/2010/06/04/would-you-like-to-go-a-gnome-accessibility-hackfest-in-seville/">Alejandro said back in June</a>, next week the <a title="1st AEGIS international conference" href="http://www.aegis-conference.eu/index.htm">1st AEGIS international conference</a> will take place in Seville, Spain, and there I'll be attending, together with him, the <a title="GNOME Accessibility Hackfest" href="http://live.gnome.org/Accessibility/HackfestAEGIS2010">GNOME Accessibility Hackfest</a> that is planned to happen there as a parallel event to the conference (see <a title="GNOME Hackfest on AEGIS conference" href="http://www.aegis-conference.eu/pages/hackfest.html">AEGIS web page</a> or <a title="GNOME Accessibility Hackfest" href="http://live.gnome.org/Accessibility/HackfestAEGIS2010">GNOME live wiki</a>, for more details).

As for me, this is the first time I attend to an a11y-related hackfest and I must said I'm quite excited about it, as been there should be the perfect scenario to work hard on moving things forward related to a11y support in the <a title="WebKitGTK website" href="http://webkitgtk.org/">WebKitGTK</a> platform, which has been what I've been working on most of the time during the last months here in <a title="Igalia's website" href="http://www.igalia.com">Igalia</a>, as part of our <a title="WebKit website" href="http://www.webkit.org/">WebKit</a> team.

On top of that, this is also going for me the first time I'll meet in real life some people I usually work with in a regular basis since I started working on a11y-related stuff in <a title="WebKitGTK website" href="http://webkitgtk.org/">WebKitGTK</a>, such as the always helpful <a title="Joanmarie Diggs' blog" href="http://blog.grain-of-salt.com/">Joanmarie Diggs</a>, who patiently answers all my doubts about those <a title="WebKitGTK bugs blocking ORCA support" href="https://bugs.webkit.org/show_bug.cgi?id=25531">bugs blocking</a> <a title="The ORCA project" href="http://live.gnome.org/Orca">ORCA</a> support, for instance.

Because of all this, I have great expectations about this hackfest, both as a personal experience (from the most selfish point of view you can imagine) and as a great boost to all the work being currently done to improve <a title="The GNOME project" href="http://www.gnome.org">GNOME</a> as an <a title="List of GNOME Accessibility efforts for GNOME 3" href="http://live.gnome.org/Accessibility/GNOME3#WebKitGtk_.28support_ATK.29">accessible platform towards the GNOME 3.0 release</a>.

Can't wait!
